Black Thoughts

Since: July 2021

Black Thoughts is the bare beginnings of my dream project, combining my doctoral research with my developing skills in Computer Science to create an interface that makes exploring intellectual discourse more interesting and fun. The site essentially treats different academic papers as nodes which are linked together based on whether they share a common related author. Modeling knowledge as a 3D graph makes it easier to show how different authors and works are connected, and might even lead to cross-disciplinary collaborations which otherwise wouldn't be thought of. My ultimate goal is to expand this page into a social media site that privileges longform content and makes academic forms of knowledge more accessible.

What I Learned:

  • Working with external libraries, in this case a 3D Graph library
  • This was my (painful) introduction to fullstack web programming!

Next Steps:

  • Use RegEx to validate/sanitize Submitted Data
  • Use WebGL to make the graph more aesthetically interesting

In the very long term, I want to explore the fact that graphs, as a composite of vertices and edges, can function simultaneously as a data-structure for information and as the raw material for graphical objects, like meshes. This could lead to interesting ways to interface knowledge as a matter of form and content. For example, if there's a cluster of connected vertices that all have to do with, say, fashion history, I would want the cluster to organize itself in the shape of a dress. This way, users could get a sense of what a group of edges is about before clicking on it.
